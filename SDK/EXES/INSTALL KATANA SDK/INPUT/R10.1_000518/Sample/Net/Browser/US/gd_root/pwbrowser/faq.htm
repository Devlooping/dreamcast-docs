Getting Online
Frequently Asked Questions

 

Q: What are my options for connecting to the Dreamcast Network (DCN)?
A: You may sign up for AT&T WorldNet® Service if you do not have an Internet Service Provider (ISP). If you do have an ISP then you may use your existing ISP.
Q: Why is AT&T WorldNet Service the ‘preferred ISP’ for the DCN?
A: AT&T WorldNet Service has advanced dial-in and backbone networks that deliver superior performance. Tight integration with AT&T WorldNet Service makes registration, usage, and maintenance of DCN accounts as simple as possible. Most importantly, when online gaming becomes available the overwhelming majority of DCN game servers will be co-located on the AT&T backbone. Getting as close as possible to these servers will give you optimal gaming performance.
Q: Can I use my existing ISP?
A: Absolutely! Just enter the required dial-up and email information to set up your system. Refer to your Dreamcast Network manual for details.
Q: How much does it cost to use AT&T WorldNet Service?
A: AT&T WorldNet Service has three pricing plans available to you. Please select Sign up for AT&T WorldNet Service for details on each of these plans. We encourage you to explore the current offerings online, as you are under no obligation until you complete the registration process by entering your credit card information.
Q: Can I cancel my service at any time?
A: Yes. To cancel service, contact AT&T WorldNet Customer Care at 1-800-400-1447.
Q: Can I change my ISP at any time?
A: Yes, just as you would change providers on your computer. Check with the ISP to determine if there are any penalties for cancellation.
Q: Can I change plans within AT&T WorldNet Service?
A: You may switch to a different price plan by visiting the Customer Care section of the AT&T WorldNet web site, which is currently located at http://www.att.net/care/accounts. Changes will normally become effective on the first day of your next billing month.
Q: Will I be calling a long distance or a pay-per-minute phone number for access to the Internet?
A: With very high probability you will be assigned a local phone number for accessing the AT&T WorldNet Service. To make this possible it is important that you enter all information accurately during registration. However, in the end you are responsible for any telephone charges. So please check with your local telephone operator if you have concerns that the number(s) provided will result in any toll charges even if the number is in the same area code (local toll charges).
Q: What if I don’t have an access number for my area or I’m traveling?
A: If you are traveling, or do not have an access number in your area, you can reach AT&T WorldNet Service by using an 800 or 888 number for an extra facility charge of $0.10 per minute. This fee applies to all usage, including free hours and additional usage hours. For more information contact AT&T WorldNet Customer Care at 800-400-1447.
Q: Are there any additional costs?
A: AT&T WorldNet Service has no additional costs beyond the monthly and hourly fees as stated. Please contact AT&T WorldNet Customer Care at 800-400-1447 to get specific information on the price plans offered. If you are using another ISP, please review their policies.
Q: What are the current promotions?
A: The Dreamcast Network and AT&T WorldNet Service are currently offering a free Dreamcast Keyboard to subscribers of AT&T WorldNet Service Unlimited or Standard price plans. You will receive your keyboard within 8 to 10 weeks after registering for AT&T WorldNet Service on either the Standard or Unlimited price plans. Supplies are limited. AT&T reserves the right to substitute or terminate this offer without notice. See details during registration for the Service.
Q: How do I get my Dreamcast Keyboard?
A: If you qualify for this promotion your free keyboard will be sent to the address entered during Dreamcast Network registration in about 8-10 weeks.
Q: What if I’m a current AT&T WorldNet Service subscriber… can I still get a keyboard?
A: Please visit http://www.att.net/sega for details.
Q: Will I be able to ‘surf’ the entire Internet?
A: Yes.
Q: Are there any parent control options available?
A: No. Please see the ‘Legal Disclaimer’ document included with your Dreamcast System.
Q: Will the Dreamcast Network browser be updated? How often? How will I receive these updates?
A: Periodic upgrades will be sent via U.S. mail to the address entered during the Dreamcast Network registration. Current plans call for updates to be sent 3-4 times per year.
Q: What features does this browser support?
A:
  • HTML 3.2
  • JavaScript
  • POP3 client, SMTP client, and email notification (with standard email servers)
  • Address Book
  • IRC Chat
  • VMU file transfer

 
Q: How do I get help from Customer Support?
A: For questions regarding your Dreamcast System or Dreamcast Network Browser, please access the extensive online help facility available through your Dreamcast Network Browser or www.sega.com. If you cannot connect to the Network and are not an AT&T WorldNet Service customer, call 1-888-345-SEGA. Please note that all information available to our customer care representatives is also available to you online, and without the wait!

If your question is about AT&T WorldNet Service, including initial ISP account registration, billing questions, or network access questions (busy signals, dropped calls, etc.) then please contact AT&T WorldNet Customer Care at 1-800-WORLDNET.

If you are not an AT&T WorldNet Service customer and you have billing questions or network access questions (busy signals, dropped calls, etc.) then please contact your ISP.
